About Me

I am a master's student from Beijing Institute of Technology, supervised by Dr. Haibin Zhang and Dr. Sisi Duan. During this period, I spent several months as a R&D intern at Yangtze Delta Region Institute of Tsinghua University. Prior to that, I received my bachelor's degree from Qinghai University, under the supervision of Prof. Yong Xie.

Research Interests

My research interests lie in Theoretical Cryptography and Distributed Computing. (though I admit my recent work is not really theoretical) I am driven by fundamental questions regarding impossibility results, new models, and composability. Specific topics include:

  • Multiparty Computation (MPC)
  • Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP)
  • Byzantine Agreement and Consensus (BFT)
  • Threshold Cryptography
